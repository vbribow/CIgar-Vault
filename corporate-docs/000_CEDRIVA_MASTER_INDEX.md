## Purpose

The Cedriva Master Index is the central navigation system for Cedriva’s corporate knowledge.

Every controlled company document receives a unique document number, version, status, title, and location. This index must be updated whenever a document is created, renamed, revised, superseded, archived, or retired.

## Working Agreement

Every deliverable will be:

- One document per transfer block.
- Wrapped in a single copyable text block.
- Versioned and titled.
- Ready to paste directly into Codex or Cedriva documentation.

## Document Numbering Standard

Document numbers are permanent and must never be reused.

- **000** — Master index and document-control system
- **001–099** — Constitution, identity, philosophy, and company principles
- **100–199** — Product strategy, standards, and product governance
- **200–299** — Brand, marketing, communications, and community
- **300–399** — Operations, policies, and internal processes
- **400–499** — Commercial strategy, partnerships, and finance
- **500–599** — Technology, data, security, and architecture
- **600–699** — Research, education, history, and knowledge standards
- **700–799** — People, leadership, culture, and organizational design
- **800–899** — Legal, compliance, risk, and regulatory matters
- **900–999** — Reserved for future use

## Version Standard

- **0.1–0.9** — Working drafts under development
- **1.0** — First approved version
- **1.1, 1.2, and so on** — Minor approved revisions
- **2.0, 3.0, and so on** — Major approved revisions

## Status Standard

- **Proposed** — Identified but not yet drafted
- **Working Draft** — Being written or reviewed
- **Pending Approval** — Complete and awaiting approval
- **Approved Draft** — Approved strategic direction that may still receive editorial refinement
- **Active** — Approved and currently governing
- **Superseded** — Replaced by a newer document or version
- **Archived** — Retained for historical reference
- **Retired** — No longer applicable and not replaced

## Document Control Rules

1. Assign a document number before treating a new document as controlled.
2. Add the document to this index when its first draft is created.
3. Place the document number, version, status, owner, and last-updated date at the beginning of every controlled document.
4. Record meaningful revisions in the document’s revision history.
5. Increase the version number whenever approved content changes.
6. Never overwrite history by reusing a retired or abandoned document number.
7. Mark a document as superseded before directing the company to a replacement.
8. Keep file names stable after a document is approved whenever practical.
9. Treat this index as the authoritative register when document metadata conflicts.

Every controlled Cedriva document should begin with:

```text
Created: [Date]

```

## Index Maintenance Checklist

Before closing any document task:

- [ ] Confirm the document has a unique number.
- [ ] Confirm its title, version, and status appear in the document.
- [ ] Confirm its file name and location are correct.
- [ ] Update its entry in the Document Register.
- [ ] Update the Master Index version and last-updated date when the register changes.
- [ ] Add an entry to the Master Index Revision History.
